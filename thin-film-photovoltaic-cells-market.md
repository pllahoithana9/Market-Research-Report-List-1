<p><p>Thin film photovoltaic cells are categorized into three main types: Gallium Arsenide (GaAs), Copper Indium Selenide (CIS), and Cadmium Telluride (CdTe). GaAs cells offer high efficiency and are often used in space applications. CIS cells provide a good balance of efficiency and cost, suitable for residential on-grid applications. CdTe cells are known for their low production costs and are widely used in large-scale solar farms. Each type plays a vital role in the overall solar energy landscape, catering to different market needs.</p></p>
