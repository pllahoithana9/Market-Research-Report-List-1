<p><p>The ground cellulose market consists of two primary types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based cellulose. Wood pulp-based cellulose is derived from trees, focusing on sustainability and cost-effectiveness, making it a popular choice in various applications such as food, pharmaceuticals, and personal care products. Refined cotton-based cellulose, on the other hand, is produced from high-quality cotton fibers, offering superior purity and functionality. This type is often preferred in specialized applications requiring enhanced performance, like advanced pharmaceuticals and cosmetics.</p></p>
