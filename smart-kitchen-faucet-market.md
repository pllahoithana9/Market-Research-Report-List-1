<p><p>The smart kitchen faucet market is primarily divided into two types: touch and contactless faucets. Touch faucets require users to physically touch the spout or handle to activate water flow, offering convenience and efficiency. On the other hand, contactless faucets utilize motion sensors to detect hand movements, allowing for a hands-free experience. Both types enhance kitchen functionality, improve hygiene, and often feature advanced technology like temperature control and water conservation, catering to modern consumers' preferences for convenience and sustainability.</p></p>
