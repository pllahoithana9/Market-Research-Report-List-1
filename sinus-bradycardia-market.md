<p><p>The sinus bradycardia market encompasses various treatment options for managing this condition. Atropine is commonly used as an initial drug therapy to increase heart rate. Isoproterenol serves as a sympathomimetic agent for more persistent cases. Aminophylline and ephedrine act as bronchodilators and stimulants, respectively, also aimed at boosting heart rate. Scopolamine, while primarily an antiemetic, can influence heart activity. Pacemakers offer a long-term solution, while implantable cardioverter defibrillators provide advanced cardiac support in severe cases.</p></p>
