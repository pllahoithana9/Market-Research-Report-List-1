<p><p>The intravenous iron drugs market comprises several key types, each used to treat iron deficiency anemia. Ferric gluconate is often used for patients with chronic kidney disease, while ferric carboxymaltose offers rapid correction of iron deficiency with fewer infusions. Iron sucrose is commonly used in patients undergoing dialysis, whereas iron dextran provides a fast-acting iron solution. Each type varies in formulation, dosing, administration routes, and patient response, catering to specific clinical needs and enhancing treatment options.</p></p>
